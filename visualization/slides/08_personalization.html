Defining Personalization

"Personalization is a process that changes the functionality, interface, information content, or distinctiveness of a system as to increase its personal relevance to an individual."
— Blom, J. (2000). Personalization - a taxonomy.
Our Goal Modifying the system so that the service or assistant fits or exceeds the expectation.

Normal System

⚙️

Pre-programmed

Static rules and generic logic designed for the average user.

📉

Low Personalization

Limited mostly to recommendation algorithms and targeted ads.

👻

Invisible

Changes happen silently behind the scenes; hard to perceive.

TLDR One-size-fits-all approach with minimal dynamic adaptation.

AI Assistant (LLM)

🎲

Probabilistic

Complex, non-deterministic responses based on vast training distributions.

⚡

Real-time Adaptation

Can dynamically shift tone and output based on immediate human feedback.

🧠

Evolving Models

A spectrum of alignment techniques from pre-training to post-training.

TLDR Highly flexible systems capable of aligning deeply with individual feedback.

Human Interaction

👁️

Constant Observation

Unconsciously reading cues, context, and subtle emotional states.

🔄

Immediate Loop

Instantly sensing misunderstandings and adjusting explanations on the fly.

🤝

Mutual Alignment

A two-way street of continuous empathy and shared understanding.

TLDR The ultimate gold standard for personalized, context-aware communication.
